<h2 align="center"><code>// rest a while</code></h2>

<p align="center">
  <b>Mal0</b> is a first-year Computer Science student at the <b>New Jersey Institute of Technology</b> —<br/>
  though the contribution graph tells a slightly longer story. He builds the way other people doodle:<br/>
  constantly, quietly, and across whatever problem has his attention that week.<br/>
  Markets one day, network traffic the next, a small tool to kill an annoyance the day after.
</p>

<p align="center">
  His work runs across <b>quantitative trading systems</b>, <b>real-time threat intelligence</b>,<br/>
  <b>applied machine learning</b>, and the unglamorous tooling that holds it all together.<br/>
  He cares less about any single stack than about the <i>shape</i> of a hard problem — and he learns<br/>
  a field by building something real inside it, not by reading about it from a safe distance.
</p>

<p align="center">
  Most of what he ships, he taught himself. He's allergic to jargon for its own sake and happier with<br/>
  working code than a clever-sounding explanation. The pace is the point: thousands of commits a year —<br/>
  not for the streak, but because the next problem is always a little more interesting than resting.<br/>
  The bonfire is just where he warms his hands between runs.
</p>
